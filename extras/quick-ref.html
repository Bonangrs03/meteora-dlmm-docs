The 5 Most Important Formulas

① The Engine — Constant Product
x × y = k
x = token A in pool · y = token B in pool · k = constant
Price = y/x  ·  Buying pushes price UP, selling pushes it DOWN.
Slippage: bigger trades = bigger price impact. Ch 5
② The Hidden Cost — Impermanent Loss
IL% = 2√p/(1+p) − 1
p = price ratio (new_price ÷ entry_price)
Applies in BOTH directions. Loss is only "impermanent" until you withdraw.

③ True LP Profit
LP Profit = Withdrawal Value − Deposit Value
The only number that matters. Not "fees look good." Not APR. Real value in, real value out.
Ch 14
④ Fee APR (Annualized)
APR = (Fees ÷ Position Value) × 365
Use daily fees for the most recent picture. Historical APR ≠ future APR.
0.1% daily ≈ 36% annual if sustained. Ch 14
⑤ The Acid Test — HODL Comparison
Win if: LP Profit > HODL Profit
HODL Profit = Value if you just held the original tokens.
If LPing underperformed holding, you paid for the education.
Compare every time you close a position. Ch 14

10 Common Mistakes to Avoid

1
Not tracking HODL comparison. "Fees look good" isn't enough — you might still be losing vs just holding.
2
Chasing extreme APR. 1000% APR usually means extreme IL is about to wreck you.
3
Skipping token vetting. Always check Rugcheck.xyz + Bubblemaps before LPing.
4
Range too narrow for volatility. Memecoins need wide ranges — tight ranges go out-of-range instantly.
5
Ignoring total fees generated. Pool with <25 SOL lifetime fees = red flag.
6
Confusing "in range" with "profitable." You can be in-range earning fees and still losing to IL.
7
Deposit and forget. LPing requires monitoring — price moves don't wait for you.
8
Starting too big. First position should be $20-50 max. Tuition, not investment.
9
Not comparing against alternatives. If fee APR <5%, a savings account may outperform with zero risk.
10
Closing without calculating. Every close = learning opportunity. Run the HODL math.

Monthly LP Health Check Checklist

① Am I tracking PnL honestly? — HODL comparison, not just fee totals
② Strategy still right for market? — Wide for trending markets wastes capital; tight for volatile ones gets rekt
③ Right monitoring frequency? — Too little = caught off-guard. Too much = overtrading.
④ Are fees worth my time? — If you spend 2 hrs/week for $3/month, reconsider
⑤ Do I understand WHY? — A profitable position you don't understand is more dangerous than a losing one you do
